The problem

 Global warming. Global warming, in simple terms, is the long-term increase in the Earth's average temperature due to the buildup of certain gases in the atmosphere, primarily carbon dioxide from human activities like burning fossil fuels.

 For example, the majority of the electricity that we all use comes from power stations, which mostly burn fossil fuels. Fossil fuels release carbon dioxide when burnt, which is a greenhouse gas. This is only one of the many gases that human activities produce, which lead to global warming.

 Global warming is one of the biggest problems of today's world. Global warming leads to climate change, which in turn has been increasing the risk of natural hazards all around the world. Global warming also induces rising sea levels, which could engulf low-lying island territories in the future. These are just a fraction of the problems caused by global warming.

  With increasing global surface temperatures the possibility of more droughts and increased intensity of storms will likely occur. As more water vapor is evaporated into the atmosphere it becomes fuel for more powerful storms to develop. More heat in the atmosphere and warmer ocean surface temperatures can lead to increased wind speeds in tropical storms.
