Technological innovation

What is technological innovation?

 Technological innovation, in terms of global warming, is the introduction of new technologies which can work towards reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Examples include:

  • Carbon capture and storage (CCS)
  • Direct air capture (DAC)
  • Renewable energy sources
  • Energy-efficient appliances
  • Electric vehicles

How do these technolgies work?

 Carbon capture and storage (CCS) technology captures CO2 emissions from power plants and industrial facilities and stores them underground, where they cannot escape into the atmosphere. This technology is still in its early stages of commercialization, but it has the potential to significantly reduce CO2 emissions from these sources.

Carbon capture and storage

 Direct air capture (DAC) technology captures CO2 directly from the atmosphere. This technology is also in its early stages of commercialization, but it has the potential to play a role in removing excess CO2 from the atmosphere and mitigating the effects of climate change.

 Renewable energy sources, such as solar, wind, and geothermal power, do not produce CO2 emissions. By investing in renewable energy, we can reduce our reliance on fossil fuels and reduce CO2 emissions. Ocean waves are also being used to generate electricity (wave power) but this technology is still in its early stages. On the other hand, solar, wind, geothermal, and HEP have been well-known technologies for a long time and can be relied on.

 Energy-efficient appliances consume less energy than their inefficient counterparts, and this reduces the amount of fossil fuel combustion that has to take place and therefore carbon dioxide levels too.

LED lights are very efficient and cause less CO2 emissions

 Electric vehicles make use of electricity instead of combusting fuels internally. These have the potential to cause no harm at all to the environment, because the electricity used can come from renewable sources of energy.

What you can do

 Implementing energy efficiency is one of the easiest things you can do to reduce carbon emissions. Here are some examples of energy-efficient appliances that you could use:

  • LED light bulbs: LED light bulbs use up to 90% less energy than incandescent bulbs and last up to 25 times longer.
  • Energy Star appliances: Energy Star appliances are certified to meet strict energy efficiency standards. They are available for a wide range of appliances, including refrigerators, washing machines, dryers, dishwashers, and air conditioners.
  • Smart thermostats: Smart thermostats can learn your heating and cooling habits and adjust the temperature automatically to save energy.
  • Water-efficient appliances: Water-efficient appliances, such as low-flow toilets and showerheads, can help to reduce your water consumption and energy bills.
  • Appliances with built-in energy-saving features: Many appliances now come with built-in energy-saving features, such as automatic shut-off and sleep mode.

 Electric vehicles tend to be more expensive than conventional vehicles, but this is mostly because they are not a widely used technology yet. If in the financial state to buy and use an electric vehicle instead of a fuel-powered one, doing so could be very beneficial for the environment.  Finally, if possible, work with a local environmentalist group to promote renewable energy sources and technologies such as CCS and DAC and to spread awareness. These technologies have the potential to reduce carbon emissions greatly.
