Land use management

What is land use management?

 Land use is the way that the world's land is used. It may be used for farming, for buildings, for growing trees, etc.

 Forests and farms are referred to as 'carbon sinks', meaning they absorb CO2 and stop it from entering the atmosphere. This is very useful because CO2 is the most significant cause of global warming. Unfortunately nowadays, trees are cleared and replaced by buildings and such, and steps are rarely taken to offset this reduced absorption of carbon dioxide. This problem is known as deforestation.

The effects of exploitative land use

 Deforestation for urbanization and for agriculture reduces the presence of the world's most important carbon sinks, causing carbon levels that are already soaring to increase further.

Deforestation is a serious problem all over the world, and sadly Brazil, the home of the great Amazon Rainforest, suffers the most

Ways to manage land use

 Reforestation and afforestation can be effective ways to offset the effects of deforestation. Reforestation is the replanting of trees where there were once trees, whereas afforestation is the planting of trees on land where there were no trees before, i.e., creating a new forest. The newly planted trees could replace previously cleared trees and take their role of absorbing CO2.

The importance of forests

 If unable to plant more trees, the next best thing you could do would be to encourage reforestation and afforestation. This way other people could work towards offsetting deforestation.
