Reduction of other greenhouse gases

What are the other greenhouse gases?

 Despite carbon dioxide being the most significant cause of global warming, there are other contributors such as methane, nitrous oxide, and CFCs.

Methane, nitrous oxide, and CFCs (fluorinated gases) follow carbon dioxide as factors of global warming

Where are these greenhouse gases emitted?

 Methane is released in large proportions by many processes:

  • Agriculture (the digestive process of ruminant animals, manure management, and cultivation of rice release methane)
  • Production and transportation of fossil fuels
  • Decomposition of organic waste
  • Burning of biomass
Methane, the second most significant contributor to global warming, is released by numerous human activities

 Nitrous oxide is released from (ⅰ) the use of chemical fertilizers and manure management in agriculture, (ⅱ) the combustion of fossil fuels, (ⅲ) industrial chemical reactions, etc.

Sources of nitrous oxide

 CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons) and halons not only act as greenhouse gases, but also towards ozone layer depletion. The main uses of CFCs are as coolants in refrigerators and air conditioning systems and as propellants in aerosol spray cans, whereas halons are useful in fire extinguishers. The dangers of using these substances have been acknowledged (learn more about the Montreal Protocol), and they are now being used less than before.

 A large number of contries have subscribed to the Montreal Protocol and have been working towards reducing the emissions of ozone-depleting substances.

 It can be seen in the graph on the left that there has been a significant decline in emissions of ozone-depleting substances. Their emissions have gone from a whopping 1.6 million tonnes in 1989 to less than 20,000 tonnes today.

The effects of these greenhouse gases

 Similar to increased CO2 emissions, increased emissions of these greenhouse gases can also contribute to the enhanced greenhouse effect and therefore towards global warming.

 Despite carbon dioxide being the most significant contributor to global warming, methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O) also cause an increase in global temperature.

Ways to reduce these greenhouse gases

 Supporting renewable sources of energy can go a long way in reducing not only your carbon footprint, but also methane and nitrous oxide emissions as these gases are only released from fossil fuels, in terms of fuels. Renewable sources are sources of energy which can never be run out of (e.g., solar power, wind power, hydroelectric power, tidal power, etc.), whereas non-renewable sources like fossil fuels are vulnerable to depletion. Ways to support renewable sources include:

  • Using electric vehicles instead of fuel-powered vehicles
  • Choosing an energy provider that offers renewable energy options
  • Installing solar panels

 Energy efficiency also applies here. Using energy wisely, avoiding fuel-powered vehicles, and preferring carpooling or public transportation over personal vehicles are all ways to reduce the use of fossil fuels and thus also methane and nitrous oxide emissions.

 Avoiding meat can be an effective way to reduce methane emissions, because as discussed earlier, livestock produce a lot of methane.

Meat causes massive methane emissions

 Composting waste when possible can also help in reducing methane emissions from landfills.
