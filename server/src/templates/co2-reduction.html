CO2 reduction

Where is CO2 emitted?

 Carbon dioxide, i.e., CO2, is the main contributor to the enhanced greenhouse effect which causes global warming. Unfortunately, this gas is released in large proportions by various applications such as:

  • Electricity generation (from fossil fuels)
  • Fuel-powered vehicles (e.g., petrol, diesel, gas)
  • Industrial processes (production of cement, steel, etc.)
  • Agriculture (production of livestock, use of fertilizers and pesticides)

The effects of CO2

 Increased CO2 emissions since the beginning of the Industrial Revolution have set atmospheric carbon levels at a continuous rise.

Ways to reduce CO2

 One of the easiest ways to reduce your carbon footprint is reducing your reliance on fuel-powered vehicles. You may instead walk, bike, or use public transportation. Carpooling is also an effective way to reduce CO2 emissions, as more people are transported with the same amount of carbon dioxide released.

Carpooling reduces carbon emissions

 Another very easy way to reduce your carbon footprint is energy efficiency. Turn off electrical appliances when not in use. This can save electricity and therefore reduce the amount of carbon dioxide emitted in producing electricity. This method comes with the added benefit that your electricity bill may be lower.

 Nearly 70% of residential CO2 emissions come from electricity generation. As lighting accounts for nearly 10% of residential electricity use, reducing the total energy used by lighting has the potential to decrease carbon dioxide emissions. — National Science Foundation

 Supporting renewable sources of energy can go a long way in reducing your carbon footprint. Renewable sources are sources of energy which can never be run out of (e.g., solar power, wind power, hydroelectric power, tidal power, etc.), whereas non-renewable sources like fossil fuels are vulnerable to depletion. Ways to support renewable sources include:

  • Using electric vehicles instead of fuel-powered vehicles
  • Choosing an energy provider that offers renewable energy options
  • Installing solar panels

 These methods all ensure that you obtain energy with little to no carbon dioxide released into the atmosphere.

 Livestock production is also a major source of greenhouse gas emissions. Therefore, consuming less meat can be an effective way of reducing your carbon footprint.
